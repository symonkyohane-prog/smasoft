Bachelor of Science in Computer Systems and Security (2019-2024)


Malawi University of Science and Technology


Rationale for the Programme


Computer systems and security play an important role in supporting commerce, banking, telecommunication, health care and other socio-economic agendas. However, system breaches and failures have the potential to undermine the safety and viability of industrial and commercial systems, fuel criminal activity and endanger human life


While the 21st century has witnessed a technological revolution brought about by the integration of computer technology in almost every aspect of life, security challenges inherent in computer hardware and software are on the increase. Worldwide, computer systems are falling victim to a unique variety of security breaches that usually have profound negative social, economic and legal implications.


Since Malawi is not spared from falling victim to these security breaches and given the rate at which Malawi is adopting these advances in computer technology, it is imperative that a programme that will train computer systems security professionals be developed to impart necessary knowledge and skills to safeguard computer systems from vulnerabilities and security breaches.


The purpose of this programme is to provide comprehensive academic training required by security professionals in order to select, develop, implement and manage computer systems security solutions. The programme also focuses on imparting knowledge of core non-technical issues of security such as social, economic, legal, regulatory and ethical considerations and related implications.


Educational Aims of the Programme


The main aim of this programme is to produce high-caliber graduates with capabilities to understand and develop secure computer systems


Programme Learning Outcomes


(a) Knowledge and Understanding


At the end of this programme, graduates should be able to:


  • Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of computer systems

  • Discuss computer systems security concepts including cryptography, information assurance, network security, information systems security, and relevant non-technical issues including legal, health and safety, social, ethical, and economic aspects
  • Explain the various methodologies and best practices used to manage network, computer, and information systems security
  • Discuss ethical and social issues related to computer systems security



(b) Intellectual Skills


At the end of this programme, graduates should be able to:


  • Demonstrate a systematic and creative approach to problem solving

  • Apply ethical principles required of computer professionals

  • Demonstrate a holistic and systematic approach to computer systems security implementation

  • Exhibit propensity to utilize knowledge acquired in the areas of computer systems and security for innovation and entrepreneurship


(c) Practical Skills


At the end of this programme, graduates should be able to:


  • Analyse and specify the systems security needs of an organization

  • Specify and design secure systems

  • Design and undertake systems security audits and forensics

  • Implement and manage physical and technical security measures for computer systems

  • Use appropriate cryptographic technologies to secure computer systems

  • Design and implement backup and recovery procedures

  • Develop and apply computer systems security policies

  • Model and simulate computer security systems



(d) Transferable Skills


At the end of this programme, graduates should be able to:


  • Communicate effectively and persuasively using the full range of currently available methods

  • Manage resources and time
  • Work in a team, which may be multi-disciplinary

  • Learn independently, identifying own personal development needs and goals, reflecting on own performance and managing own personal development

  • Sort, manipulate, and present data in a way that facilitates effective analysis and decision making
